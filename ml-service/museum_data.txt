-----------------------------------------------------------
GENERAL OVERVIEW OF RWANDAN MUSEUMS
-----------------------------------------------------------
Rwanda is known as the "Land of a Thousand Hills" (Pays des Mille Collines). It has a rich cultural heritage spanning thousands of years, from the ancient monarchy of the Mwami (King) to the tragic events of 1994 and the extraordinary national rebirth. Rwanda's museums preserve this layered history for future generations and international visitors.

The major museums in Rwanda are:
1. King's Palace Museum (Nyanza, Southern Province)
2. Ethnographic Museum (Huye, Southern Province)
3. Museum Ingabo (Rebero Hill, Kigali)
4. Campaign Against Genocide Museum / CAG (Parliament Building, Kigali)
5. Kandt House Museum / National History Museum (Nyarugenge, Kigali)
6. Rwanda Art Museum (Kanombe, Kigali)

Visitors to Rwanda's museums can expect to learn about pre-colonial royal culture, traditional arts, the 1994 Genocide against the Tutsi, and Rwanda's amazing post-genocide recovery. The museums support multilingual guided tours in English, French, and Kinyarwanda.

-----------------------------------------------------------
MUSEUM 1: KING'S PALACE MUSEUM (RUKARI)
Location: Nyanza District, Southern Province, Rwanda
Opening Hours: Daily from 9am to 5pm
-----------------------------------------------------------

Welcome to the King's Palace Museum! This section contains all knowledge about the King's Palace in Nyanza.

HISTORY AND ARCHITECTURE:
The King's Palace Museum, or "Rukari," is the historical seat of the Rwandan monarchy. The site features a reconstructed traditional palace, an architectural masterpiece of 3D beehive-shaped dome design. 
- **The Rugo (Compound)**: A circular enclosure made of reed and bamboo.
- **Kambere (Main Residence)**: The King's primary beehive hut, constructed entirely without nails using ancient weaving techniques.
- **Specialized Huts**: 
    - **Milk Hut**: Historically guarded by virgin girls to ensure the purity of the royal milk.
    - **Beer Hut**: Managed by male virgins, dedicated to the production of traditional honey and sorghum beer for the court.

INYAMBO CATTLE (Royal Sacred Cows):
The Inyambo are distinguished by their lyre-shaped horns spanning over two meters. They are "royal poets" trained to perform a graceful parade in response to traditional flute music and praise songs. They are a living symbol of Rwanda's regal heritage.

SACRED DRUMS:
The **Karinga Drum** was the "heart of the nation," ranked equal to the King himself. It was carved from the *Umuvugangoma* tree ("the tree that calls for drums").

MUSEUM 2: ETHNOGRAPHIC MUSEUM (HUYE)
Location: Huye District, Southern Province
-----------------------------------------------------------

One of Africa's finest collections. Its seven galleries cover:
- **Daily Life**: Ancient basketry (Agaseke), tools, and clothing.
- **Craftsmanship**: The iconic zigzag pattern on Agaseke baskets represents "Two women holding hands," symbolizing reconciliation.
- **Music**: The *Inanga* zither, carved from a single piece of wood.

-----------------------------------------------------------
MUSEUM 3: MUSEUM INGABO (KIGALI)
Location: Rebero Hill, Kigali
-----------------------------------------------------------

Founded by artist King NGABO in 2023.
- **Inzira y'Inzitane (The Labyrinth)**: 30 metal sculptures telling the story of the 1994 Genocide and Rwanda's renaissance.
- **The Blind Drum Walk**: A sensory experience where visitors are blindfolded and lead by the sound of rhythmic drumming.

-----------------------------------------------------------
MUSEUM 4: CAMPAIGN AGAINST GENOCIDE MUSEUM (CAG)
Location: Parliamentary Building (CND), Kigali
-----------------------------------------------------------

Documents the RPA's 100-day campaign to stop the genocide.
- **The 600 Soldiers**: The brave RPA soldiers who were besieged inside this building while protecting thousands of civilians.
- **The 12.7mm Gun**: The iron guardian mounted on the rooftop used to repel genocidal forces.
- **Third Battalion Sick Bay**: A preserved site where the wounded were treated during the siege.
- **Outdoor Trenches**: Recently added exhibit showing the actual bunkers used by the 600 soldiers.

-----------------------------------------------------------
MUSEUM 5: KANDT HOUSE MUSEUM (NATIONAL HISTORY MUSEUM)
Location: Nyarugenge, Kigali
-----------------------------------------------------------

Former residence of Dr. Richard Kandt, who found the source of the Nile in Nyungwe Forest.
- **Colonial History**: Documents the transition of Kigali from a hilltop post to a national capital.
- **Natural History**: Extensive taxidermy of Rwandan birds and mammals.

-----------------------------------------------------------
MUSEUM 6: ENVIRONMENT MUSEUM (KARONGI)
Location: Karongi (Kibuye), Western Province
-----------------------------------------------------------

The only environment museum in Africa, housed in a building originally intended as a palace for King Mutara III Rudahigwa.
- **Rooftop Herbal Garden**: Features 127 species of indigenous medicinal plants and trees.
- **Geological Displays**: Volcanic rocks, soils of Rwanda, and meteorites moved from the Kandt House.
- **Renewable Energy**: Exhibits on hydropower, solar, and the methane gas extraction from Lake Kivu.

-----------------------------------------------------------
MUSEUM 7: KIGALI GENOCIDE MEMORIAL (GISOZI)
Location: Gisozi, Kigali
-----------------------------------------------------------

A final resting place for 250,000 victims.
- **Exhibitions**: Traces the history of Rwanda from pre-colonial times to the 1994 Genocide against the Tutsi.
- **Children's Memorial**: A heart-wrenching tribute to the youngest lives lost.
- **Gardens of Reflection**: Spaces for meditation and remembrance.

COMMON QUESTIONS
-----------------------------------------------------------

Q: What is the significance of the 600 soldiers at CND?
A: They were RPA soldiers who defied odds to protect civilians inside the Parliament building during the 1994 Genocide.

Q: Where can I see traditional medicinal plants?
A: The Environment Museum in Karongi features a famous rooftop garden with over 120 indigenous plant species.

Q: Who created the Imigongo art?
A: It originated in the Gisaka kingdom, created by Prince Kakira using cow dung and natural pigments.
